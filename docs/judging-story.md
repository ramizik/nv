# Judging Story

## The 10-second hook
> "A $16,000 veneer lead calls your closed clinic at 9pm. Today: voicemail, gone. With us:
> the agent answers, qualifies, scores it HOT, alerts your front desk, and drafts the
> callback — entirely on the Dell GB10, on-prem."

## The business problem (why judges care)
Cosmetic dental is high-ticket and competitive. A single veneer case is $7k–$16k; a smile
makeover is $12k–$30k. Clinics lose these leads to:
- **After-hours calls** nobody answers
- **Busy front desks** that miss or rush calls
- **Inconsistent qualifying** — staff forget to ask about timeline/financing
- **Slow follow-up** — first responder wins; competitors call back faster

## Our solution
An always-on, on-prem agent that handles the **first interaction** instantly, privately,
and consistently — and hands staff a qualified, scored, ready-to-act lead.

## Why local / on-prem (the Dell × NVIDIA GB10 angle)
- **Privacy:** patient health conversations never leave the building — no cloud, no BAA risk.
- **Always-on & cheap per-call:** no per-token cloud bill on every after-hours call.
- **Latency:** reasoning in ~100–150ms on the GB10.
- This is exactly the "always-on business agent running locally" the track asks for.

## ROI math (say this out loud)
- One recovered veneer case ≈ **$7k–$16k**.
- Miss two a month after hours = **$170k–$380k/yr** of lost pipeline.
- The agent recovers a meaningful slice of that, on hardware the clinic already owns.
> "One recovered case pays for the box many times over."

## What's genuinely agentic (not just a chatbot)
- **Extracts** structured slots from open conversation
- **Reasons** against *this clinic's* rules (financing, lead-time, premium-lead policy)
- **Decides** a score + urgency with confidence
- **Acts** across surfaces: posts the alert, drafts the follow-up, sets a callback task
- **Shows its work** in an operator dashboard (no raw chain-of-thought)
